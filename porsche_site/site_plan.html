Content

Home page

Welcome to "Porsche Picker," an online hub for exploring the rich and captivating history of the Porsche 911. This is a space for enthusiasts, by an enthusiast, dedicated to exploring, celebrating, and comparing every iteration of this legendary sports car. Whether you're a seasoned owner, a prospective buyer, or simply an admirer of automotive excellence, our goal is to provide a comprehensive and engaging platform to understand the nuances that make each 911 generation a masterpiece in its own right.

911

The connection to the very first 911, initially designated the "901," is immediately apparent in the 1970 model's silhouette. This was the era of the classic "long hood," a design characterized by its graceful, uninterrupted slope from the windshield to the bumper. This elegant and simple form factor, free of the heavy, impact-absorbing bumpers that would arrive in 1974, is a direct link to Ferdinand "Butzi" Porsche's original 1963 design. The slim profile, delicate chrome trim, and iconic round headlights are all hallmarks of this foundational period. Inside, the car retains a minimalist and driver-focused cabin. The five-gauge instrument cluster, with the tachometer placed squarely in the center, established a design language that continues to this day. There is an elegant simplicity to the controls and a sense of direct mechanical connection that was a hallmark of the first 911s.
